Pre-Workout

If your BMI (Body Mass Index) is less than 18.5, it's essential to focus on exercises that can help you gain muscle mass and strength while promoting overall health. Keep in mind that you should also pay attention to your diet to ensure you're getting enough nutrients to support your fitness goals. Here are some pre-workout exercises that can be beneficial for individuals with a BMI below 18.5:

1.Bodyweight Exercises: These exercises use your body weight as resistance, helping you build strength without the need for heavy equipment.

a. Push-ups: Great for building chest, shoulder, and triceps strength.
b. Bodyweight Squats: Strengthen your legs, glutes, and core.
c. Lunges: Target your legs and improve balance and stability.
d. Planks: Engage your core muscles and work on stability.

2.Resistance Band Exercises: Resistance bands are lightweight and versatile tools that provide resistance during exercises.

a. Bicep Curls: Strengthen your biceps using resistance bands.
b. Shoulder Press: Work on your shoulder muscles.
c. Seated Row: Target your back and arm muscles.

3.Dumbbell Exercises: Dumbbells are great for adding resistance and promoting muscle growth.

a. Dumbbell Bench Press: Strengthen your chest and triceps.
b. Dumbbell Shoulder Press: Target your shoulder muscles.
c. Dumbbell Lunges: Add resistance to your lunges for better leg strength.

4.Bodyweight Cardio Exercises: Enhance your cardiovascular fitness with bodyweight movements.

a. Jumping Jacks: Increase your heart rate and warm up the body.
b. High Knees: Engage your core and work on leg strength and coordination.
c. Mountain Climbers: Build endurance and work multiple muscle groups.

5.Flexibility and Mobility Exercises: These will help you improve your range of motion and prevent injuries.

a. Dynamic Stretching: Perform controlled movements that mimic the exercises you'll do during the workout.
b. Foam Rolling: Help release tension in muscles and improve mobility.

6.Yoga and Pilates: Both practices can improve flexibility, core strength, and overall well-being.

Remember to start any exercise program gradually and listen to your body. If you have any pre-existing health conditions or concerns, consult with a healthcare or fitness professional before starting a new exercise routine. They can provide personalized guidance based on your specific needs and goals.
